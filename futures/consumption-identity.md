Noah Smith argues that AI abundance could shift the basis of personal identity from production to consumption — and that this would be liberating, not empty. Consumption choices are more individuating than work because they force you to ask "what do I want?" rather than "what does the market need?" College is the existence proof: a period where identity comes from taste, exploration, and self-expression rather than career.
