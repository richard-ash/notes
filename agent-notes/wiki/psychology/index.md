# Psychology
Applied psychology, behavioral self-management, motivation, and the science of human decision-making.

## Articles
- [[psychology-of-human-misjudgment]] — Munger's 25 psychological tendencies that cause systematic misjudgment, with practical antidotes and the lollapalooza principle
- [[cialdini-influence]] — Cialdini's six principles of influence (reciprocity, commitment, social proof, liking, authority, scarcity), how compliance professionals weaponize them, defensive heuristics, and the lollapalooza link to Munger's broader catalog
- [[personal-productivity-systems]] — why all productivity systems decay, context intentionality, modified pomodoro, and strategies for renewal
- [[vulnerability-and-shame]] — Brown's research on shame as the fear of disconnection, the wholehearted traits, and why we cannot selectively numb emotion
- [[ai-art-contamination]] — AI involvement in art triggers psychological contamination: trace AI usage causes disproportionate devaluation, and exclusivity premiums collapse for AI-made work
- [[probability-neglect]] — Lamade's "Achilles heel" essay: base-rate neglect as our defining cognitive blind spot (the valedictorian/Ivy math), why marketers exploit it (Munger's 20%-returns anecdote), the inversion that the bias is load-bearing for irrational-looking achievement, and the "challenge the odds when the pursuit matters as much as the outcome" heuristic
- [[dialogic-thinking]] — Skoczeń's "Dialogue Dividend": understanding-a-problem is social where implementing-a-decision is solitary; verbalization-forces-precision + a-listener-supplies-feedback mechanisms, the Mercier-Sperber/Vygotsky/Clark-Chalmers scaffolding for reasoning-as-social, the relational dividend of unscheduled exchange, and why LLM sycophancy delivers only half the dividend by default
- [[speed-compounds]] — Kuhn's "Be impatient": impatience as a productive disposition, the convergence of speed→effectiveness across email/startups/tools/negotiation/combat/life, and the load-bearing mechanism — being fast lets you *process information* faster so you end up doing *different* things, which compounds the growth rate of output (Boyd's OODA loop, Elhage's faster-tools-change-usage); with the correlational caveat and the irreversible-decision exception
- [[high-agency]] — Cate Hall's "manifest determination to make things happen" as a *learnable* skill: agency as finding real edges others won't take (poker reads as the origin), the six hacks (court rejection, seek anonymous feedback, maximize surface area for luck, assume every trait is learnable, love the moat of low status, don't work too hard), reduced-agency as an early burnout signal, and the tension with [[shut-up-and-do-the-impossible]]'s effort-maximalism
- [[not-automatically-strategic]] — Salamon's LessWrong essay: being strategic is a learned skill, not a human default; a fitness-landscape "most actions fail by default" argument, weak-goals (story/role/feeling) vs. real-goals, the eight heuristics we don't auto-run (name the target, define the success signal, be curious, gather info, experiment, exploit, verify the goal is yours, engineer motivation), the dual-process mechanism (verbal systems outrun the motivational systems that pull behavior, and retraining them is itself non-automatic), and the everyday-omissions footnote (salary comparisons, typing tutors, base rates); the problem statement that became CFAR
- [[shut-up-and-do-the-impossible]] — Yudkowsky's ladder of effort (tsuyoku naritai → isshokenmei → extraordinary effort → "shut up and do the impossible"), the trying-vs-winning distinction, "impossible" usually meaning "I see no path", holding full-impossibility and intent-to-solve in the same mind while refusing every exit from the tension (faith, seizing-any-solution, certainty-of-uncertainty), the AI-Box Experiment as worked example, and the warning that you are allowed to lose
