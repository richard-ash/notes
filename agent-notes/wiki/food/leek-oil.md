# Leek Oil

A zero-waste condiment that transforms the dark green tops of leeks — usually discarded or relegated to stock — into a vibrant, emerald-green finishing oil with deep earthy-umami flavor. The technique is essentially a blanch-blend-strain process that takes 15 minutes of active work but benefits enormously from a 12–24 hour maceration before straining.

## Why it works

Blanching the leek tops first accomplishes two things: it softens the tough cell walls for easier blending, and it sets the bright green chlorophyll color (the same principle behind blanching herbs for pesto or green goddess dressing). Blending with oil then extracts the fat-soluble flavor compounds. The extended resting period allows more of those compounds to migrate into the oil, producing a markedly more robust result than immediate straining.

## Recipe

| | |
|---|---|
| **Yield** | ~1 cup |
| **Active time** | 15 min |
| **Maceration** | 12–24 hours (optional but recommended) |
| **Diet** | Vegan |

### Ingredients

- 1½–2 cups leek tops, sliced
- 1 cup light olive oil or grapeseed oil (avoid bitter or strongly flavored oils)
- 4 cups water + 1 tsp salt (for blanching)
- ¼ tsp salt (for blending)

### Method

1. Bring salted water to a boil. Blanch leek tops for 90 seconds until vibrant green and tender.
2. Drain, shock under cold water for 30 seconds, and blot dry with paper towels.
3. Blend leek tops, oil, and salt until fairly smooth — leave some visible particles. Over-blending makes straining difficult.
4. **For maximum flavor:** let the mixture sit covered at room temperature for several hours, or refrigerate overnight (12–24 hours).
5. Strain through a fine-mesh sieve, pressing solids with the back of a spoon.

### Key tips

- **Don't over-blend.** Small visible particles are fine and make straining much easier.
- **Use mild oil.** Light olive oil or grapeseed — not extra-virgin, which can turn bitter.
- **Save the solids.** The strained leek paste works in soups, stews, dressings, or spread on bread.

## Storage

- **Refrigerator:** 7–10 days in a sealed glass jar.
- **Freezer:** Freezes well for longer storage (ice cube trays work for portioning).

## Serving ideas

Drizzle over [[potato-leek-soup]], corn chowder, risotto, pasta, roasted vegetables, hummus, or crusty bread. Works anywhere you'd use a finishing herb oil.
