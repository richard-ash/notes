# Red Lentil Bulgur Soup

A Lebanese-style soup of split red lentils and coarse bulgur in a paprika-tomato base, finished with a drizzle of garlic-mint olive oil. Maureen Abood adapts a recipe originally from *The Washington Post*. Serves 6 in about 30 minutes; vegetarian and vegan when made with vegetable broth.

## Why this works

**Split red lentils** are the structural choice. Because they are halved (split across the middle), they have no seed coat to soften and they break down quickly into a creamy purée — no soaking, no par-cooking, fully tender in under 30 minutes. Whole brown or green lentils hold their shape and would produce a different soup entirely; Abood is emphatic that they are not substitutes here. The same property is why red lentils are the body of dal but are unsuitable for mujadara-style pilafs that need the lentils to stay intact.

**Coarse bulgur (#3)** is parboiled cracked wheat. It thickens and adds chew where the lentils have gone creamy — an important texture contrast in an otherwise smooth soup. Fine bulgur (#1) would dissolve; coarse bulgur cooks in roughly the same 20–25 min window as the lentils.

**The mint oil** is the flavor-maker that elevates this from a weeknight pot to something Abood calls "company-worthy." It follows the same pattern as the [[leek-oil]] finishing drizzle on [[potato-leek-soup]]: a separately prepared aromatic oil added at the bowl, not the pot, so the volatile aromatics aren't cooked off. Dried mint blooms in warm oil; fresh mint would not give the same depth.

## Recipe overview

| **Time** | ~33 min total (5 prep, 28 cook) |

### For the soup

- 2 tbsp extra virgin olive oil
- 1 medium yellow onion, diced (~1 cup)
- ½ tsp kosher salt, plus more to taste
- 2 tbsp minced garlic (~5 cloves)
- 2 tsp paprika
- ⅛ tsp cayenne pepper
- 2 tbsp tomato paste
- 1½ cups diced tomato (fresh or from a 14–15 oz can)
- ½ cup coarse bulgur, #3 grade
- ½ cup red lentils, sorted, rinsed, and drained
- 2 cups chicken or vegetable broth
- 3 cups water
- Juice of 1 lemon
- Several grinds black pepper

### For the mint oil

- 3 tbsp extra virgin olive oil
- 2 tsp crushed dried mint (or Maureen Abood Market's "Garlic Mint Salt")
- Pinch cayenne pepper
- 1 clove garlic, minced

### Method

1. Heat olive oil in a large pot over medium heat until it shimmers. Add onion and ½ tsp salt; cook ~5 min until translucent.
2. Add garlic, paprika, cayenne, and tomato paste. Cook ~1 min until fragrant.
3. Add diced tomatoes, raise heat, and boil ~3 min, stirring occasionally.
4. Stir in bulgur, lentils, broth, and water. Return to a boil, cover, reduce to medium-low, and simmer 20–25 min until lentils and bulgur are cooked through but not mushy.
5. Stir in lemon juice and black pepper. Taste and adjust salt.
6. **Mint oil:** in a small sauté pan over low heat, warm the 3 tbsp olive oil. Add minced garlic and cook ~1 min until aromatic. Off heat, stir in the dried mint and a pinch of cayenne.
7. Ladle soup into bowls and finish each with a generous drizzle of mint oil.

### Recipe-card note

The source's prose lists a 25-min simmer in step 4; the printable recipe card says 20 min and (presumably a typo) lists "bulgur" twice in the ingredient stir-in. Treat 20–25 min as the window — pull when the lentils have collapsed but the bulgur still has bite.

## Variations Abood endorses

- **Heat:** swap red pepper flakes for cayenne; or omit entirely for a non-spicy version.
- **Herbs:** fresh cilantro, oregano (for a Greek lean), or curry powder for complexity.
- **Greens:** stir in baby spinach in the last few minutes.
- **Greek finish:** crumbled feta on top in place of (or in addition to) the mint oil.

The fixed elements are the split red lentils, the coarse bulgur, the lemon at the end, and the finishing oil.

## What to serve with it

A leafy salad and crusty bread make it a meal. Abood pairs it with garlic knots brushed with za'atar.

## Storage

Not addressed in the source, but the soup will thicken substantially as the bulgur and lentil starches set. Thin reheats with broth or water.
