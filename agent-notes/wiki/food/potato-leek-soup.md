# Potato Leek Soup

A classic French-inspired soup built on two humble ingredients — leeks and potatoes — blended into a creamy, comforting bowl. This version from Sylvia Fountaine emphasizes simplicity (30 minutes, pantry staples) while adding a clever zero-waste technique: turning the normally discarded dark green leek tops into a finishing leek oil.

## Key technique: using the whole leek

Most recipes discard the dark green tops. Fountaine's approach renders them into a vibrant **[[leek-oil]]** by blanching and blending the tops with olive oil, then straining. This oil serves as both garnish and flavor amplifier — a good general technique for any allium-based soup.

## Recipe overview

| | |
|---|---|
| **Yield** | ~8 cups (6 servings) |
| **Time** | 30 min total (10 prep, 20 cook) |
| **Method** | Stovetop, immersion blender |
| **Diet** | Vegetarian; vegan-adaptable |

### Core ingredients

- 3–4 large leeks (4–5 cups sliced), white through pale green parts
- 1½ lb Yukon Gold potatoes, thinly sliced (or any thin-skinned potato; peel russets)
- 3 tbsp olive oil (or butter/ghee)
- 4 cloves garlic
- 1 tbsp fresh thyme (or 1 tsp dried)
- 4 cups vegetable broth or chicken stock
- ½ cup sour cream or heavy cream (see vegan notes)
- Salt, pepper, fresh chives for garnish

### Method

1. Slice and rinse leeks. Reserve dark green tops for leek oil if desired.
2. Sauté leeks and garlic in oil over medium-low heat, 8–10 min until tender and golden.
3. Add potatoes, broth, salt, pepper, and thyme. Bring to a boil, then cover and simmer 15–20 min until potatoes are fork-tender.
4. Blend with an immersion blender until mostly smooth — **do not over-blend** or the starch will turn gummy.
5. Whisk in sour cream or cream. Adjust seasoning.
6. Serve with chives, a drizzle of leek oil, and crusty bread.

### Vegan adaptation

Skip the dairy entirely (the potato starch provides body), or use vegan sour cream. Alternatively, simmer ¼–½ cup raw cashews with the potatoes, blend together, then finish with a drop of vinegar or lemon juice and nutritional yeast for tanginess.

## Storage

- **Refrigerator:** 4 days in an airtight container.
- **Freezer:** Up to 6 months — freeze *without* the dairy, then stir in sour cream after reheating.
