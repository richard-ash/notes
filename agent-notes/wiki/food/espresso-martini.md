# Espresso Martini

The espresso martini is a vodka-based cocktail combining fresh espresso, coffee liqueur, and simple syrup. Despite its popularity, it has a reputation for being inconsistently prepared at bars and restaurants — making it a good candidate for home preparation, where the freshness of the espresso is easier to control.

## Recipe (serves 2)

### Ingredients

- 3 oz fresh espresso
- 4.5 oz vodka
- 1.5 oz Kahlua (coffee liqueur)
- 1.5 oz simple syrup
- 6 coffee beans for garnish

### Method

1. Combine espresso, vodka, Kahlua, and simple syrup in a cocktail shaker filled with ice.
2. Shake vigorously for 2–3 minutes until the mixture is well-chilled and frothy.
3. Strain into martini glasses. Garnish with three coffee beans per glass.

## Key technique: the foam

The signature crema-like foam on top comes entirely from vigorous shaking with plenty of ice. Freshness of the espresso is the main variable — freshly pulled espresso produces significantly more foam than cooled or pre-made coffee. Kelly from Kelly's Clean Kitchen describes the threshold simply: "You want your arms to be tired."

## Substitutions

- **Espresso**: Coffee can substitute in a pinch, but the drink loses concentration and foam. If no home espresso machine is available, buying fresh espresso from a coffee shop the same day works well.
- **Vodka**: Tequila or gin can replace vodka, though both shift the flavor profile noticeably.
- **Simple syrup**: Equal parts white sugar and water, heated until dissolved. Can be made ahead and refrigerated.
