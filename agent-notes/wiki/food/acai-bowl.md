# Acai Bowl

An acai bowl is a thick smoothie bowl made from frozen açaí (pronounced *ah-sigh-EE*) palm fruit puree, originating in Brazil. The base is blended until thick and ice-cream-like, then served in a bowl with toppings. Traditional Brazilian versions use granola, banana, and guaraná syrup, though savory variations with shrimp or dried fish also exist in parts of the country.

## The fruit

Açaí berries grow on palm trees in South American rainforests. About 80% of the berry is seed — the nutritional value is concentrated in the remaining pulp. The berry is unusual among fruits: higher in fat and lower in sugar than most. Per 100g of frozen unsweetened pulp: 70 kcal, 5g fat, 5g carbs, 4g fiber, 0g sugar, 1g protein.

Despite widespread "superfood" marketing, the evidence for açaí-specific health benefits beyond those of other berries is thin — WebMD notes "no known health benefit that's different from similar fruits."

## Recipe (serves 2)

**Ingredients:**
- 200g (2 packets) frozen unsweetened acai puree
- 1 banana, sliced and frozen
- ½ cup frozen blueberries
- ½ cup frozen strawberries
- ¾ cup milk or juice (dairy, plant-based, or fruit juice)
- ½ cup plain yogurt (optional — sub with more liquid if skipping)

**Method:**
1. **Freeze fruit** — pre-freeze banana slices, blueberries, and strawberries on parchment-lined tray (or use store-bought frozen fruit)
2. **Blend** — add liquid and yogurt to a high-speed blender first, then frozen fruit and broken-up acai packets. Blend on low, using a tamper to push fruit down. Add liquid sparingly — thickness is the goal
3. **Top and serve** — divide into two bowls, add toppings (granola, fresh fruit, nuts, seeds, coconut). Eat immediately — it melts

**Per serving (base only):** ~248 kcal, 29g carbs, 7g protein, 10g fat, 7g fiber, 20g sugar

## Key tips

- A blender with a **tamper** is almost essential for achieving proper thick consistency. Without one, you'll need more liquid and get a thinner result.
- Use **unsweetened** acai puree — sweetened versions mask the natural flavor.
- Skip flavored yogurt — plain only, or the acai taste disappears.
- Don't add sweetener, especially with banana in the mix.
- Start blender on low and work up gradually to avoid burning out the motor.
- **Vegan adaptation:** swap dairy milk for plant-based milk or apple juice, use plant-based yogurt or skip it, and check granola labels (many contain honey).

## Health considerations

The base is reasonably nutritious, but toppings can escalate calorie counts quickly. Randhawa notes that many social-media acai bowls marketed as "healthy" are closer to dessert once loaded with granola, nut butters, and honey. Using a smaller bowl and checking granola nutrition labels are practical mitigations.
