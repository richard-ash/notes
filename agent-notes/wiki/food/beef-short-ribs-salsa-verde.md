# Beef Short Ribs in Salsa Verde

A Mexican braised beef dish where bone-in short ribs are slow-cooked until fall-apart tender, then finished in a fresh tomatillo-serrano salsa verde. The technique is a two-stage braise: first in plain broth to break down the collagen, then in the salsa so the meat absorbs the bright, tangy flavors without the sauce losing its freshness to hours of cooking.

## Ingredients

- 4 pounds beef short ribs
- 1/2 pound tomatillos milperos (smaller, more intensely flavored than standard tomatillos)
- 4 serrano peppers
- 4 garlic cloves
- 1/4 onion
- Small bunch cilantro
- Salt and pepper
- 2 1/2 cups beef broth (divided: 1 1/2 cups for the braise, 1 cup for the salsa)

## Method

1. **Sear** — Season ribs with salt and pepper. Brown on all sides in a large heavy pot.
2. **First braise** — Add 1 1/2 cups broth, cover, and cook on low heat for 3–4 hours until the meat is nearly fork-tender.
3. **Make the salsa** — Blend tomatillos, serranos, garlic, onion, cilantro, 1 cup broth, and a pinch of salt until smooth. (Can be done while the ribs braise.)
4. **Second braise** — Remove the ribs, optionally skim fat from the pot, pour in the salsa verde, return the ribs, cover, and simmer 45 minutes to 1 hour until the meat shreds easily.

If the liquid runs low at any point, add more broth rather than letting the pot dry out.

## Notes

- **Tomatillos milperos** are a small wild variety of tomatillo with a more concentrated, slightly sweeter flavor. If unavailable, standard tomatillos work — you may want to increase the quantity slightly.
- Four serranos will produce moderate-to-significant heat. Scale down to 2 for a milder result.
- The two-stage approach is the key insight: braising directly in salsa verde for 4+ hours would dull the salsa's brightness. Adding it only for the final hour keeps the green, herbal flavor alive while still letting it permeate the meat.
