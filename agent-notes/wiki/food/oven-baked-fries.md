# Oven Baked Fries

The key to crispy oven-baked fries is **par-boiling** the potatoes before roasting. A 5-minute boil in salted water draws out excess surface starch — the main culprit behind soggy oven fries. This is the same principle behind many restaurant-style fry techniques (double-cooking in some form), adapted here for home ovens without deep frying.

## Method

1. Peel and cut 4 russet potatoes into thick steak fries.
2. Boil in heavily salted water for 5 minutes. Drain well.
3. Toss with olive oil, salt, pepper, garlic powder, and smoked paprika.
4. Spread in a single layer on a parchment-lined sheet tray.
5. Roast at 415°F for 20 minutes, flip, then roast another 15 minutes.

**For extra crispiness:** use the bottom oven rack, where radiant heat from the heating element is strongest.

## Notes

- This technique works specifically with **white/russet potatoes**, which are high-starch. Sweet potatoes have much less starch and don't benefit from par-boiling.
- The recipe also works well in an air fryer (adjust time down as air fryers run hotter with more convection).
- Par-boiling serves a dual purpose: it removes starch *and* partially cooks the interior, so the oven time is spent crisping the exterior rather than cooking through.
