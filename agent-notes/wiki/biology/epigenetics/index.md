# Epigenetics

Epigenetic mechanisms, aging clocks, chromatin regulation, epigenetic reprogramming, and theories of aging.

## Articles
- [[developmental-theory-of-aging]] — de Magalhães's hypothesis: aging as run-on of the developmental software program, not hardware damage; explains species scaling, longevity interventions, and reprogramming via a single framework
- [[epigenetic-clocks]] — CpG methylation-based models predicting age and mortality; first-gen (Horvath, Hannum) vs. second-gen (PhenoAge, GrimAge); causality debate; validation by CR, rapamycin, and dwarf mice
- [[in-vivo-epigenetic-reprogramming]] — In vivo partial reprogramming across CNS tissues: Lu et al. (eye, OSK, vision restoration) and Rodriguez-Matellán et al. (dentate gyrus, cyclic OSKM, H3K9me3 restoration and memory improvement); TET1/TET2 requirement; safety of cyclic vs. continuous dosing
- [[information-theory-of-aging]] — Sinclair's ITOA: aging as progressive loss of epigenetic information, validated by the ICE mouse model (non-mutagenic DSBs cause whole-organism aging), reversible via OSK partial reprogramming; the search for the cellular "backup copy"
- [[somatic-restriction-theory]] — West et al.'s framework: aging as progressive developmental repression of regenerative potential through four staged transitions (PT, EFT, NT, AT) via antagonistic pleiotropy; proposes induced tissue regeneration (iTR) as therapeutic strategy
