# Eli Lilly

Eli Lilly is the world's most valuable pharmaceutical company (~$800B market cap as of late 2025), driven primarily by its GLP-1 franchise (Mounjaro/Zepbound). Under CEO Dave Ricks (11th CEO, joined 1996 as BD/M&A analyst), the company has built a distinctive model combining internal R&D productivity, direct-to-consumer distribution, and a willingness to target $0 markets.

## Why the premium valuation

Lilly trades at ~50x trailing P/E vs a sector average of ~12. The delta is explained by:

1. **GLP-1 dominance**: ~80% of the company's economic value. Capturing 70-75% of new patient starts in the US, 60/40 total share vs Novo Nordisk. Revenue growing ~40% YoY.
2. **R&D productivity premium**: investors believe each R&D dollar creates more value at Lilly than at peers. Most pharma R&D spending is viewed as value-destructive by the market.
3. **Franchise value thesis**: the possibility that GLP-1s could create a durable self-pay consumer brand that outlasts the patent cycle — more like Apple's ecosystem than traditional pharma's boom-bust.
4. **Rule of 80**: one of only three large-cap companies (alongside NVIDIA and Broadcom) with margin + growth exceeding 80.

Key patent timelines: tirzepatide US patent extends to late 2030s; oral orforglipron extends beyond that.

## LillyDirect: the direct-to-consumer play

### Origin story
When the head of US commercial operations retired, Ricks took the role himself alongside being CEO. The idea of a direct pharmacy was initially shelved ("pharmacies will hate us; we know nothing about running one"). It was pulled off the shelf when a pharmacy chain threatened not to carry Lilly's low-price insulin.

### Evolution
1. **Insulin**: ensure access at lowest price. Small volume.
2. **Migraine medicine**: struggling with insurance coverage. Slightly more volume.
3. **Zepbound**: the "killer app" — diagnosis requires only a bathroom scale, telehealth for prescribing, self-monitoring.

### Current state
- Annualizing in the billions of dollars
- Largest prescription platform online by revenue
- Runs on Stripe
- **Zepbound self-buy is the #1 prescribed form of GLP-1** — more new patient starts than the insured business, more than all of Wegovy

This represents a fundamental shift: for 150 years, Lilly's relationship with patients was mediated by pharmacies and insurers. LillyDirect creates a direct relationship where patients pay $500/month by credit card and skip the entire "this is not a bill" morass. The model works because GLP-1s have a wide therapeutic index and self-evident biomarker (weight).

## R&D model: hybrid integration

### Internal discovery
- Distributed in satellite labs of ~300-400 people (San Diego monoclonal antibody hub produced 1/3 of medicines since launch)
- "Allowable variation": unsanctioned projects tolerated and funded when they show promise
- "Let them cook" philosophy — don't manage budgets to the nickel
- Innovation is iterative: GLP-1 story spans 2006 to present, with years of protein engineering grinding

### External
- Corporate venture with ownership stakes in satellite biotech companies
- **Catalyze360 / Gateway Labs**: host scale-up biotechs (not startups), providing active Lilly scientists rather than retired consultants. 7-8 locations including two in China and one building in London.
- Watch list of every clinical/preclinical readout and patent posting globally
- When acquiring biotechs: buying target validation, often redoing the invention loop internally

### Philosophy
Lilly targets **three types of R&D** simultaneously:
1. Franchise extension (next indication for existing drugs)
2. Moonshots on $0 markets (conditions with no existing treatment)
3. Platform investments (new drug modalities that unlock entire classes of targets)

Ricks holds R&D at 20-25% of revenue as a principle. At projected revenue of $120B, that would approximate the entire NIH budget.

## Organizational culture

- **150 years old**, turning 150 in May 2026
- 11 CEOs in that period — "one less than popes." Only one was an external hire.
- Internal succession: people rotated through roles they "had no business being in" to develop breadth
- Ricks started in BD/M&A, ran China, ran US business, ran development before becoming CEO
- Currently keeping **headcount flat** while revenue grows 40%: R&D spend growing high teens/low 20s, R&D headcount growing single digits
- Uses AI aggressively: Ricks runs "at least one or two AIs" during every meeting for science questions; prefers Claude and xAI over ChatGPT ("too verbal," references don't check out)

## China competition

Lilly views China's rise in biotech (0% → 30% of global pipeline in a decade) with concern but nuance:
- China excels at "iterative derivative" — refining and replicating known approaches
- Truly novel concepts require a local ecosystem that China hasn't yet proven
- The patent clone problem (1-2 atom differences) is real and threatens the industry
- But: "I totally respect their desire to build this" — health security is a legitimate national interest
