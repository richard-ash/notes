# AOH1996

AOH1996 is a small molecule inhibitor of the cancer-associated isoform of proliferating cell nuclear antigen (caPCNA), developed at City of Hope. It represents a first-in-class approach to cancer therapy: rather than directly damaging DNA or blocking a single enzymatic pathway, it exploits **transcription-replication conflicts (TRCs)** — collisions between the transcription and DNA replication machineries — as a cancer-selective vulnerability.

## Mechanism of action

PCNA is a homo-trimeric ring that encircles DNA and serves as the central hub of the replisome. It interacts with dozens of replication and repair proteins through its PIP-box and APIM binding domains on the outer hydrophobic surface adjacent to the inter-domain connector loop (IDCL).

AOH1996 binds into the PIP-box cavity of PCNA (confirmed by co-crystal structures at 2.81 A and 3.77 A resolution). Its key molecular effect is to **stabilize the interaction between PCNA and RPB1**, the largest subunit of RNA polymerase II (RNAPII). Normally, when transcription and replication machineries collide on a chromosome, PCNA helps dislodge RNAPII from the conflict site so the replication fork can proceed. AOH1996 prevents this displacement by locking the PCNA-RPB1 interaction, leading to:

1. **Persistent unresolved TRCs** — the replication fork cannot pass through the conflict site
2. **R-loop accumulation** — the unfinished RNA transcript forms a DNA:RNA hybrid structure
3. **DNA double-strand breaks** — catastrophic and lethal to the cell
4. **RPB1 proteasomal degradation** — further disrupting the transcription machinery

Critically, this entire cascade is **transcription-dependent**: the transcription inhibitor DRB suppresses AOH1996-induced DNA damage, and RPB1 Y418A APIM-domain mutants (which weaken RPB1-PCNA interaction) confer resistance. RNase H1 overexpression (which resolves R-loops) also partially rescues the damage.

## Cancer selectivity

AOH1996 achieves remarkable selectivity (>30-fold) between cancer and normal cells. The median GI50 is approximately 300 nM across more than 70 cancer cell lines, while nonmalignant cells (PBMCs, small airway epithelial cells, neural crest stem cells) show no significant toxicity up to at least 10 uM. It does not alter cell-cycle profiles of normal cells or induce apoptosis in them.

Two factors explain this selectivity:

- **Cancer cells have more TRCs.** Fast-growing cancer cells run both transcription and replication at high rates, and oncogene-driven firing of replication origins further dysregulates the spatiotemporal segregation of these processes during S-phase. Normal cells have fewer collisions to exploit.
- **Cancer-associated PCNA isoform (caPCNA).** A distinct post-translationally modified form of PCNA found in cancer cells disrupts the PCNA-TRC interface, making cancer cells specifically vulnerable to AOH1996.

Because PCNA is a non-oncogene addiction target (essential for cancer cell survival but not itself oncogenically mutated), it may be less prone to resistance development than oncogene-targeted therapies.

## Drug properties

AOH1996 was derived from an earlier hit compound (AOH1160) through crystallography-directed medicinal chemistry. A methoxy group added to the meta position of the terminal phenyl ring protects against CYP-mediated hydroxylation, improving metabolic stability:

- **Oral bioavailability:** ~90% absorption in a clinically compatible formulation (Kolliphor EL / Poloxamer P124)
- **Half-life:** 4.33 hours (mouse, 40 mg/kg oral) — a 27% improvement over AOH1160
- **Safety:** NOAEL >=250 mg/kg/dose BID (mouse) and 75 mg/kg/dose BID (dog) in GLP toxicity studies — more than 6x the effective dose. No discernible toxicity, no weight loss, not a genotoxic mutagen (Ames test negative)

## Preclinical efficacy

AOH1996 significantly reduced tumor burden as monotherapy in xenograft models of neuroblastoma, breast cancer, and small cell lung cancer. It showed synergy with existing chemotherapies:

- **Cisplatin:** synergistic cancer cell killing in clonogenic assays
- **Topotecan:** synergistic growth inhibition
- **CPT-11 (irinotecan):** combination treatment increased median survival by 55.4% vs untreated controls (statistically significant over either agent alone)

Pharmacodynamic markers identified in treated tumors include focal gamma-H2A.X and phospho-Chk1 staining, consistent with DSB induction and checkpoint activation.

## Clinical status

As of the paper's publication (2023), AOH1996 had entered **Phase 1 clinical trials**. City of Hope holds patents on AOH1996 and its analogs. The compound's IND-enabling toxicology studies were filed and accepted by the US FDA.

## Connections

AOH1996 sits at the intersection of several important themes in modern oncology:

- The broader strategy of targeting [[pharma-industry-economics|non-oncogene addiction pathways]] — exploiting cancer dependencies on stress-response machinery rather than oncogenes themselves
- The growing recognition that **replication stress** is a fundamental hallmark of cancer, not just a byproduct
- The underexplored biology of transcription-replication conflicts, where AOH1996 serves as both a therapeutic candidate and a research tool for dissecting TRC resolution mechanisms
