- [[ai-eats-the-world]] — Evans's Spring 2026 deck plus May 2026 Lenny's Podcast: three pillars (Capital, Deployment, Change) — $700bn capex, mobile-network analogy and the commodity-models thesis, distribution as the moat in commodity infra (browser-wars parallel, Google/Meta spraying Gemini/Meta-AI on every surface, Apple's WWDC 2024 vision as the load-bearing on-device case), mile-wide-inch-deep consumer adoption, pilots-not-production enterprise, why frontier labs are hiring forward-deployed engineers (FDE = "Accenture outsourced engineer in SF" — enterprises have no spare cycles to reimagine workflows themselves), probabilistic-on-deterministic as the new general-purpose technology, the AGI-definition drift (Tesler: "AI is whatever machines can't do yet"), the anti-AI backlash decomposed (water claim ~0.017% fake, energy real but bounded, jobs data inconclusive, creative-industry culture wars real, UK Post Office/Horizon scandal as the every-tech-wave-ruins-lives template), and "it'll probably be okay" + dive in
- [[ai-code-review]] — Lawson's slow-AI-coding stance and ensemble bug-finding technique: multi-model PR review (Claude + Codex + Cursor Bugbot) with critical/high/medium/low ranking, near-zero false positives, the pre-existing-bug side quest as codebase-health subsidy, and AI velocity reinvested into quality rather than throughput
- [[decide-execute-deliver-sandwich]] — Narayanan's argument that AI won't cause mass software-engineering layoffs: work is a decide-execute-deliver sandwich and AI compresses only the middle; the layoff stories are "AI washing" (Block/Snap/Intuit, NY WARN-Act checkbox, 21%-vs-2% HBR gap); writing code was never the bottleneck (8× more code → 30% more releases); vibe coding ≠ agentic engineering; and price-elasticity may grow engineer demand
- [[enterprise-agentic-coding-adoption]] — Airbnb's DPE Summit 2025 field report (Faber & Nakhimovich): the vision-and-work-backwards rollout, ~64% of engineers materializing code via agents, the holistic three-lens measurement (sentiment + adoption + aggregate PR velocity), the four-rung skill ladder with auto-approve discipline (hold test code to prod quality, push back on the agent), parallel sessions needing cloud sandboxes over git worktrees, the IDE-first → CLI-first → invest-in-the-system arc, the Airchat vendor-agnostic wrapper + early MCP bet (12+ internal servers, paved-path auth), the failed orchestrator detour ("can't beat them, join them" → delegate to the CLI engine), "tool-calling is all you need" re-wrapping internal RAG as an MCP tool instead of fine-tuning, and six lessons (it takes a village, don't call CLI an underdog with 80% shifting CLI-ward, remove barriers, don't force editor migrations, standardize-but-not-prematurely, don't drop your standards)
- [[patron-not-wizard]] — Mollick's "patron, not wizard" framing for Claude 5 Fable (the first public "Mythos-class" model): the headline shift is relational, not just capability — autonomous models that run for hours, spawn their own sub-agent fleets (cheaper Sonnet researchers + adversarial verifier groups), and make hundreds of unsupervised judgement calls collapse the human role from *doing* to *commissioning*; steering ≠ doing, work moves from process to outcome, and the black box may be the permanent price of the power; with limits (2× Opus token cost, over-eager security guardrails downgrading to 4.8 Opus, persistent Claudisms) and the more-coders-not-fewer aside
- [[ml-research-craft]] — Vivek's "research is a stack of trainable sub-skills" essay: pick your own problems (Schulman's backward-from-the-outcome mode manufactures originality), taste as a trainable predict-then-correct muscle, upgrade inputs (old papers underpriced, read the paper not the thread, range beats depth), write everything down (Graham/Feynman/Darwin → a hypothesis-result research log), tighten the loop (research speed = speed of discovering you're wrong, tooling as first-class work, Karpathy's overfit-one-batch), stare at the outputs (Ng's read-100-failures, data bugs fail silently), tune baselines until it hurts + ablate, and compounding daily edges
- [[ai-lab-economics]] — swyx's structural account of AI labs: model labs (close to GPUs/research) vs agent labs (close to use case), subscription subsidy as marketing not P&L (gym-whales metaphor), why lab-built apps are buggy (model-lab engineers don't respect the app layer), GPU annual cycle as the actual cadence of capability progress, iterative deployment as deliberate slow takeoff aligned with Feldman's vaccination hypothesis, and "sincere yearning for science fiction" as the field's selection criterion — plus Shaughnessy's subsidy-unwind bear case (per-seat subsidy → enterprise governance forces metered API repricing → open-weights escape valve via free Chinese frontier models caps pricing power → already-negative margins leave labs dependent on capital that can flee; kill switch is China going closed-source, escape hatch is AI enabling genuinely new value)
