# Business > Strategy

Business strategy, platform economics, competitive dynamics, and company case studies.

## Articles

- [[aggregation-theory]] — Thompson's framework for how zero-cost distribution and transactions let aggregators commoditize supply and own demand, plus the AI-era update on opportunity-cost compute
- [[ai-platform-moats]] — Evans's argument that foundation model companies lack the network effects, lock-in, and ecosystem dynamics of true platforms, making OpenAI's strategic position far weaker than its scale suggests
- [[competitive-moats]] — Neumann's structural taxonomy of moats (state, special know-how, scale, system rigidity), why startups have none of them at founding, the "uncertainty as temporary moat" thesis, and Griffin's practitioner observations on durability, supply/scarcity, and asymmetric bets
- [[network-effects]] — Griffin on network effects and critical mass as Munger-lattice mental models: taxonomy, Metcalfe's-law critique, the bootstrap problem, why most "network effects" are actually weak groove-in effects, and why tipping doesn't guarantee the tipper wins
- [[marketplace-rake]] — Gurley's argument that excessive platform commissions are strategically self-defeating: case studies of Booking.com, oDesk, Apple, and Facebook, plus the Booking-style "low base rake + opt-in placement auction" pattern
- [[data-content-loops]] — Kwok on the Rich Barton playbook: aggregate withheld industry data, publish a page per entity, capture search as the cold-start acquisition channel — kindling for demand-side network effects (Expedia, Zillow, Glassdoor)
- [[strategy-execution]] — Vermeulen on why most "execution failures" are formulation failures: a real strategy is a small set of mutually reinforcing choices (Hornby), paired with bottom-up experimentation (Intel) and selection by design rather than executive whim
- [[entrepreneurial-profit]] — Neumann's recap of Schumpeter: surplus profit comes only from innovation, decays as imitators arrive, and the area under that curve generates Porter's generics, moats, blue oceans, and the BCG matrix — plus the Type-1 vs. Type-2 startup distinction
- [[startup-uncertainty]] — Neumann via Knight: why high-growth startups exist at all — uncertainty (unquantifiable, uninsurable) is the only moat at founding, because incumbents are structurally forbidden by their justification regimes from acting in it; Apple and Google as case studies of converting an uncertainty window into a real moat before it closed
- [[coca-cola]] — 140-year case study in capital-light franchising, lifestyle advertising, and scale economics: from patent medicine to $300B global brand
- [[snapchat]] — Spiegel's strategic arc for Snap: close-friends > network size, software-isn't-a-moat → ecosystems and hardware, AI making distribution the binding consumer-tech constraint, and the Loonshots dual-organization model with design as an intentional bottleneck
- [[outcome-based-pricing]] — Taylor's case for paying software vendors per business outcome (resolved case, closed sale) rather than per seat or per token: the AdWords-style alignment shift that AI agents make both feasible and necessary, plus why incumbents struggle to follow
