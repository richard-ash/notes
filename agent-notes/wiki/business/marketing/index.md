# Business > Marketing

Advertising theory, brand strategy, and the mechanisms of consumer persuasion.

## Articles

- [[category-creation]] — Butterfield's 2013 Slack memo: when no one is shopping for what you've built, sell the behavioral transformation (horseback riding) rather than the product (saddles); five operational principles for marketing from both ends pre-PMF
- [[credibility-based-selling]] — Naval (2026) and HN's goatherders (2020) converge on "sales is a byproduct of credibility/usefulness, not tactics": Naval's eight steady-state moves (yes-and, charisma as confidence+love, lead-don't-manage, hunt-together stag hunts, feed-your-obsessions, fundraise on internal-excitement, walk away from bad deals, focus on upside not splits) + goatherders' cold-start protocol (four-motivations taxonomy time/money/sex/peace-of-mind, sell aspirin not vitamins, write birthday cards, introduce people, expect nothing in return)
- [[cultural-imprinting]] — Simler's theory that brand ads work by altering shared cultural meanings (enabling social signaling) rather than by conditioning individual emotions
- [[distribution-strategy]] — Griffin's curation of Thiel/Andreessen/Bezos/Wilson/Gurley/Ellison on why poor distribution (not poor product) is the #1 cause of business failure, plus Horowitz's `f(p,t)=c` channel-design framework and the product/target matrix that determines viable channel shape
- [[doorman-fallacy]] — Sutherland's name for replacing a human with technology without consequence: the role gets defined down to its measurable function and all unmeasured value (error-handling, discretion, social presence) is deleted for free — illustrated by rozumem's QR-code-brunch where self-pay apps destroyed parallelism, coordination, and discretion that human service had absorbed
