# Politics

Civil liberties, governance, state power, and political philosophy.

## Articles
- [[freedom-to-transact]] — 6529's argument that financial transaction freedom is a prerequisite for all other constitutional rights, prompted by Canada's Emergencies Act freezing protest donors' accounts
- [[ideas-as-infrastructure]] — Rajagopalan's thesis that classical liberal ideas (free trade, rule of law, property rights) are infrastructure requiring active intellectual maintenance, and that business leaders have dangerously neglected this investment
- [[capitalism-and-democracy]] — Geloso & Tabarrok's empirical and theoretical case that democracy and capitalism are mutually reinforcing open-access orders (refuting both the "democracy undermines capitalism" and "capitalism undermines democracy" theses), while *socialism* is the regime that's actually incompatible with democracy
- [[political-economy-of-reform]] — Djankov, Glaeser & Shleifer's empirical refutation of Olson's sclerosis hypothesis using 3,590 regulatory reforms across 189 countries: rich countries reform *more* (not less); reform prevalence tracks the political *capacity* to compensate losers, not the social returns to reform; veto points explain why technological reforms succeed where legal reforms (especially in labor) stall
- [[abundance-agenda]] — The Klein/Thompson/Dunkelman supply-side liberalism program one year on: vibes-A+, legislation-B, outcomes-failing; the "abundance mullet" populist synthesis (Mamdani, Sherrill); the unresolved "voice but no veto" arbitration problem; Klein's reframe that institutions are not the point — delivery is
- [[ballot-qualified-endorsers]] — Elmendorf & Schleicher's election-reform proposal: certify 3–4 organizations per cycle via voter petition as ballot-qualified endorsers entitled to label one candidate per race directly on the ballot — restoring within-party policy competition that congressional-gerrymander incentives have killed off in state legislative races
- [[proportional-representation]] — Drutman's case for multi-member-district open-list PR + House expansion as the structural fix for post-Rucho/post-VRA gerrymandering warfare and the two-party doom loop: replaces the gerrymanderable single-member district with proportional allocation, re-enfranchises the structural minority party in every state, enables multi-party competition that breaks the binary demonization spiral, with fusion voting as the adjacent mechanism for single-winner offices
- [[nyc-mayoral-election-design]] — Kapadia's diagnosis of why NYC produces weak mayoral candidates: off-year scheduling + late-June primary (turnout collapses when private schools let out) + closed primary together let the Democratic nomination (and thus the mayoralty) be won with tens of thousands of votes in a city of 8 million. Fixes: move on-cycle, normalize primary timing, open the primary. The candidate-side criterion: "extraordinarily scarce" optimism; Mike Bloomberg as most-underrated mayor case study
